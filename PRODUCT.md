# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

G&K Software serves technical founders, product leads, engineering managers, and operations leaders who need a small senior development partner. Visitors are evaluating whether G&K can design, build, stabilize, or modernize software systems without the process overhead of a large agency or the ambiguity of staff augmentation.

## Product Purpose

The website positions G&K Software as a direct, technical, dependable development shop. It should make the company feel capable across several concrete practices: product engineering, backend systems, data automation, internal platforms, modernization, and technical rescue. Success means a qualified buyer can quickly understand what G&K builds, where the team fits, and how to start a focused conversation.

## Brand Personality

Direct, technical, dependable. The voice should be plainspoken and specific, with founder-led credibility and practical engineering judgment. It should sound like experienced builders explaining how they work, not a generic software vendor trying to sound bigger than it is.

## Anti-references

The site should not look or read like a generic SaaS landing page. Avoid fake dashboard art, vague growth claims, broad transformation language, decorative metric strips, and template-like service grids that could fit any development agency.

## Design Principles

1. Make the practices concrete. Every route should describe real work G&K can perform and the buyer situations that make the service relevant.
2. Lead with senior engineering judgment. The site should make founder involvement and hands-on technical breadth visible without inflating claims.
3. Keep navigation obvious. Buyers should be able to move from homepage positioning to a relevant practice page in one step.
4. Use proof carefully. GitHub profiles and founder bios can support credibility, but copy should not overclaim beyond public evidence or known company facts.
5. Prefer direct language over polish theater. Copy should name systems, risks, artifacts, and delivery patterns instead of using generic agency or SaaS vocabulary.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use semantic HTML, readable contrast, keyboard-friendly navigation, descriptive link labels, and responsive layouts. Motion should not be required to understand the page, and any animation should respect reduced-motion preferences.
